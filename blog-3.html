Spices are aromatic plant substances used to flavor, preserve, and enhance food. Derived from seeds, bark, roots, and fruits, spices are central to global cuisine and culture.

üå± Spice Farming

Spices are typically grown in tropical and subtropical regions. India, Indonesia, Sri Lanka, and Vietnam are major producers of spices like cardamom, pepper, and cloves.

After harvesting, spices are sun-dried, cleaned, ground, or packed whole. Proper drying ensures long shelf life and aroma retention.

Spices should be stored in airtight containers, away from heat, light, and moisture. Whole spices retain flavor longer than ground ones.
